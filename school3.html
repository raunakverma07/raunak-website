Phase 3: Transition & Final Years

(Class 9 - 12)

This phase felt quite different and, in many ways, more challenging than the earlier ones. In Class 9, many of my close friends had already left the school. i moved to the Narayan Bagh branch of Sandipani. I also had to adjust to a completely new environment.

The atmosphere there was not very comfortable at first. Many students were not disciplined, and the environment felt unfamiliar and difficult to adapt to. Because of this, interactions with teachers also remained mostly formal. They maintained distance and treated students in a strict manner, focusing primarily on academics rather than personal connection.

In Class 10, the COVID-19 pandemic disrupted everything. Schools were closed, and classes shifted to an online mode. There was uncertainty about board exams, and at one point, it felt like exams would be conducted, so I prepared seriously. However, due to the pandemic situation, exams were eventually replaced with general promotion.

Class 11 did not function fully due to ongoing disruptions. When classes resumed, one of my old friends rejoined the school, and I also made some new friends. However, due to the pandemic, many activities and experiences that usually define school life could not take place, ...and eventually I progressed into Class 12.

During Class 12, I was selected as the Sports Captain based on my performance in sports. It was a memorable experience as many of my close friends were also House Captains, which made our coordination and leadership even stronger. Together, we handled responsibilities, planned events, and also enjoyed the freedom that came with the role. From organizing Sports Day to managing activities, we successfully conducted everything in a well-structured way. Along with responsibilities, this phase was also filled with fun moments that made it even more special.

Around the same time, 16 August 2022 became a memorable date for me — the day I met someone for the first time whose presence quietly stayed in my memories. Even though things didn’t go beyond that moment, the experience remained meaningful. Later, this friendship gradually faded and eventually broke, leaving behind only a formal connection. I hope that with time, things may naturally become comfortable and normal again

Board exams taught me the real value of even a single mark. I scored 374 out of 500, while students with 375 marks received a government reward of ₹25,000. That single mark difference made me realize how small margins can create significant outcomes.

Overall, this phase included a mix of challenges, new friendships, life lessons, and meaningful experiences. It marked the end of my school journey — what I consider the “Best Phase of Life.” Even though it ended, it left behind memories, growth, and lessons that will always stay with me.
